Briefing for New ICU Staff

Internal guidance — please read carefully and act promptly.

Welcome — we're delighted you'll be joining us in the ICU. As part of your introduction to critical care, we aim to make it possible for you to attend The Critical CARE Course — an excellent two-day programme that will set you up well for your placement. Please read this document carefully and act on it promptly, as places are limited and some steps take time to arrange.

Please complete all five steps below as soon as possible. We cannot guarantee a place on the course or shift cover if this is left too late.

Your Action Checklist

1. Apply for Study Leave

If you have study leave allowance, apply for this in the usual way as soon as possible. No ICU signatures are needed before submitting — simply note on the form that attendance is with permission from Prof Hulme to attend the Critical CARE Course at the start of your placement.

2. Book your place on the course

You don't need to wait for your shift cover to be confirmed — visit the course website and secure your place as soon as you can, as places are limited: thecriticalcarecourse.com. Pay upfront — you will be reimbursed through study leave funding. If a shift cover issue comes up after you've booked, we will still reimburse you. If you do not have access to a study leave budget, please get in touch with Prof Hulme before you book your place, as soon as possible.

3. Notify the ICU roster team

Once you've booked your place, email the rostering team to let them know and request that your shift is covered or taken off the roster: swb-tr.SWBH-GM-anaes-rota@nhs.net

4. Complete your pre-course reading — Day 1 of the course

Work through the course manual before the face-to-face teaching day. The SWB education team considers this preparation day to be Day 1 of the course. It is expected to be completed during Self Development Time or in your own time.

The course usually takes place a couple of weeks after you start with us, and previous resident doctors have found it helpful to do some ICU learning before they start on the unit, so they have a better understanding from the outset. The course manual serves that purpose too, as well as making the face-to-face day more valuable.

5. Submit your claim for reimbursement — after attending the course

Once you have attended the course, submit the relevant claim for the course fee in the usual way to recover it via your study leave funding.

About the Course

The Critical CARE Course runs over two days: Day 1 is self-directed preparation using the course manual (considered by the SWB education team as the first day of the course), followed by a face-to-face teaching day. The taught day builds directly on the manual, so it is essential you complete the pre-course reading beforehand and arrive with a good grasp of the background material.

Course website: https://thecriticalcarecourse.com

Please note: The Critical CARE Course is an external course — SWB simply facilitates places. Booking is handled through the course's own web-based system. Once you have paid, course information is sent to you automatically. If it doesn't arrive, check your junk folder first (NHS email filters are often the culprit). If you still haven't received it, contact the course organisers directly.

Study Leave & Reimbursement

The course fee is reimbursable through study leave funding via the standard SWB process. You will need to:

  • Apply for study leave in the usual way.
  • Book and pay for your place on the course website.
  • Submit a claim for the course fee in the usual way after attending the course.

Six weeks' notice is not required for this course — the education team is aware of this. You do not need ICU signatures before submitting. Simply note on the form that attendance is with permission from Prof Hulme to attend the Critical CARE Course at the start of your placement.

If you do not have access to a study leave budget, please get in touch with Prof Hulme straight away.

Shift Cover

SWB expects and supports attendance at the course as far as is reasonably possible, and will try to arrange cover around your shifts. Please note, though, that we cannot guarantee attendance at the course for all residents — sorry. The department's usual approach is:

  • Those who are not on call during the day can be released from the roster.
  • For those on call, we aim to provide locum cover, but you would have to pay back the on-call at a later date.
  • Those scheduled to be on call the night before should arrange a swap for the night on call and then take the following day as study leave. If there is enough notice, we will ask you to pick up a vacant slot, and advertise their on-call as a locum shift.

Please note: if you claim paid study leave and are granted time off (e.g. through shift rearrangement), you will be expected to repay the clinical time at a later stage.
